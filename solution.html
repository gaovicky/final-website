Tips



  • Leave 10-15 minutes between calls to take a break. Bathroom breaks do not count. Stand up and walk around.


  • Take “audio only” breaks. Not only is it important to turn off your camera, but you should also turn your body away from the screen “so that for a few minutes you are not smothered with gestures that are perceptually realistic but socially meaningless.”


  • Make sure your meeting has an end time and stick to it.


  • Adjust your computer settings to reduce the size of people’s faces


  • “Hide self-view” if you are able to


  • Use bluetooth headphones or earbuds, so you can turn off the camera and walk around








  • Suggest an audio call instead of a video call


  • Use email or chat messages to reduce meetings. Save video conferences for meetings that require more work.


  • Using an external keyboard and external camera can create more room for you to doodle and pace in virtual meetings just like real meetings.
